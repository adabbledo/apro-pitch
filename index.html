The gap

No one owns the supply chain.

Regulated ingredients cross borders governed by drug treaties, import permits, GMP standards, and market authorisations that change by jurisdiction. There is no single system connecting compliant supply to manufacturing to distribution. The harder the regulation, the worse the fragmentation.

Brands
Need compliant manufacturers, lab-tested supply, licensed distributors, and regulatory-cleared imports. Every jurisdiction has different rules for what they can sell and how.
Manufacturers
Need certified labs, qualified suppliers, licensed distributors, and compliant packaging and labelling. A single product crossing one border can require dozens of documents.
Cultivators
Need brands to buy their product, manufacturers to process it, distributors to move it, and export permits for every jurisdiction. Most have no visibility into who needs what.
Labs & Pharmacies
Labs need manufacturer samples flowing. Pharmacies need product catalogs. Clinics need prescribing pathways. All of it is jurisdiction-dependent and none of it is connected.
Everyone's waiting for the market to open. Nobody's building the infrastructure for when it does.
