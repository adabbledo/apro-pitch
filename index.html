The problem

The supply chain runs on
spreadsheets and phone calls.

International pharmaceutical and CPG supply chains are some of the most regulated systems on earth. Ingredients move across borders governed by drug treaties, import permits, GMP standards, and market authorisations that change by jurisdiction.


Nobody has a single system that connects compliant cultivation to manufacturer to distributor. Suppliers can't find buyers in new markets. Manufacturers can't verify supply chain compliance without weeks of manual diligence. The harder the regulation, the worse the fragmentation.

Five-plus source countries. Each with different export frameworks, quality standards, and treaty obligations.
Every jurisdiction is different. What's compliant in Australia fails in Germany. What passes in Canada gets rejected in Japan.
The harder the product, the bigger the gap. Controlled substances, novel ingredients, scheduled goods — the toughest categories have the worst infrastructure.
Everyone's waiting for the market to open. Nobody's building the infrastructure for when it does.
The timing

The US market is moving toward federal legalisation of cannabis. When that happens, every international brand, cultivator, and manufacturer will need compliant supply chains overnight. The companies that already have the map win.

The product

A compliance engine
disguised as software.

On the surface, it's a GMP-compliant manufacturing system — batch records, electronic signatures, quality management, label control, traceability. Everything a regulated manufacturer needs to operate and pass audit.


Underneath, it's a supply chain network. Every company that signs up becomes a node. Cultivators, manufacturers, distributors, brands — all connected through a compliance layer that maps who can supply what to whom, in which market, under which regulatory framework.

Manufacturing intelligence
GMP batch records, electronic signatures, quality gates, deviation tracking, CAPA, equipment management, audit trails. Built for Part 11 compliance from line one.
Supply chain mapping
Jurisdiction-aware compliance engine. Knows which suppliers are authorised for which markets. Maps the shortest compliant path from raw material to finished product.
Regulatory intelligence
Multi-jurisdiction regulatory framework. Market authorisations, import/export requirements, treaty obligations, labelling rules — all in one system, all current.
Label & document control
Dynamic label generation per jurisdiction. Document management with version control, training records, and compliance-ready export for any auditor in any market.
It doesn't look like cannabis software. That's entirely the point.
The business model

Two revenue streams.
One network effect.

Manufacturers subscribe.
The platform is a tool first. Manufacturers pay for the compliance engine — batch records, quality management, regulatory intelligence. It runs their operation. This is the anchor.

Every manufacturer who signs up adds demand to the network. They need compliant suppliers. They need distribution channels. The platform knows where to find them.
Suppliers pay for visibility.
Cultivators, raw material suppliers, primary producers — they want to be found by manufacturers in markets they can't reach alone. They pay to be listed as verified, compliant supply chain partners.

They get an account. The account is easy. The system is intuitive, compliant, and actually useful. They start using it. Then they want the full platform wherever they operate.

The more manufacturers join, the more valuable supplier listings become. The more suppliers join, the more useful the platform is for manufacturers. The network compounds.
