The gap

No one owns the supply chain.

Regulated ingredients cross borders governed by drug treaties, import permits, GMP standards, and market authorisations that change by jurisdiction. There is no single system connecting compliant supply to manufacturing to distribution. The harder the regulation, the worse the fragmentation.

Brands
Need compliant suppliers, licensed manufacturers, certified labs, reliable distributors, and pharmacy relationships. Can't verify any of it from a single system.
Manufacturers
Need qualified suppliers, certified labs, approved cultivators, distribution partners, and brand contracts. Every relationship requires separate compliance paperwork.
Cultivators
Need brand offtakes, manufacturer contracts, distributor access, and pharmacy channels. Each jurisdiction demands different certifications for the same product.
Labs & Pharmacies
Labs serve every node — manufacturers, brands, cultivators, importers. Pharmacies need product supply, clinic referrals, and distributor links. Everyone depends on them, nobody connects them.
